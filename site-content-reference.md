**Full post body (reconstructed from retrieved content):**

Hey, everyone! Today I want to talk about the difference between viral and bacterial infections.

Viral infections are caused by tiny particles that invade your cells and use them to make more viruses. Common examples include colds, flu, chickenpox, and COVID-19. Most viral infections have no cure and require the immune system to respond and clear the infection.

Bacterial infections come from living organisms that can multiply on their own. Examples include strep throat, urinary tract infections, and tuberculosis. Bacterial infections may respond to antibiotics, though overuse of antibiotics creates resistant strains.

Viral infections typically present with: fever, cough, sore throat, and body aches.
Bacterial infections typically show: pus, swelling, redness, and localised pain.

The author recommends: hand-washing, covering coughs and sneezes, and staying home when sick.
